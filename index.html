Reduce Plastic, Restore the Oceans

Cleaning up plastic to save our planet

"There are 5.25 trillion pieces of plastic floating on the sea surface. It all adds up to 269,000 tons. That is heavier than 11 Statues of Liberty"

National Geographic

Have you ever been to a beach, only to find that it's littered with pieces of trash? That trash is called ocean pollution, and it's only getting worse.

Every year, more than 11 million metric tons of plastic circulate our oceans, adding to the 200 million metric tons of plastic already there. The high amounts of trash in the ocean greatly contribute to a multitude of enviormental problems that our world struggles with today.

The Death of Marine Life

It is estimated that 100,000 marine animals are killed every year due to the consumption of plastic material or entanglement with plastic material. 81 out of 123 marine animal species are known to have been affected by ocean pollution, including 7 species of sea turtles. Learn More

Global Warming

Through its degradation, plastic pollution produces methane, a greenhouse gas that is 21 times stronger than carbon dioxide. The methane from plastic pollution is slowly released into the atmosphere, contributing to the rise of global warming. Learn More

CO2 Emissions

As it is slowly broken down within ocean water, plastic pollution produces the greenhouse gas carbon dioxide, also known as CO2. CO2 is relased into the atmosphere through plastic pollution, contributing to the growth of CO2 emissions on a global scale. Learn More
