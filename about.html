What is Ocean Pollution?

Ocean pollution refers to the contamination of our oceans by harmful substances and debris, primarily resulting from human activities. This pollution includes plastics, chemicals, oil spills, agricultural runoff, and sewage, which degrade water quality, harm marine life, and disrupt ecosystems. The pervasive issue of plastic pollution, where millions of tons of plastic waste enter the oceans each year, poses significant threats to both wildlife and human health, highlighting the urgent need for global action to protect our seas. The Solution

Fortunately, it isn't too late to save our oceans. Utilizing eco-friendly ways of living, we can preserve marine life and improve our enviorment. If we take action to limit and remove the amount of pollution within the oceans, we can save millions of lives, including our own.
